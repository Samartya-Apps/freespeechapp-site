## Why We Built FreeSpeech

Every child deserves to communicate. But for children with language disabilities — whether from autism spectrum disorder, Down syndrome, or developmental language disorder — the path to expressive language can feel impossibly steep.

Traditional approaches to grammar instruction rely heavily on text and rote repetition. For children who think and communicate through images rather than words, these methods create barriers rather than bridges.

FreeSpeech was built to remove those barriers.

## The Picture-Tile Idea

The core insight is simple: children with language disabilities often have rich mental worlds full of ideas, but they lack the linguistic scaffolding to express those ideas as sentences.

What if we could meet them where they are — in the world of images — and gently guide them toward grammatical structure?

That's exactly what FreeSpeech does. Children select picture tiles representing nouns, verbs, and describing words, and arrange them into sentences. The app provides immediate, non-punishing feedback when the structure isn't quite right, and celebrates success when it is.

## Built With Clinicians

FreeSpeech was co-designed with a certified Speech-Language Pathologist who works directly with children using AAC (Augmentative and Alternative Communication) systems. Every feature reflects real clinical needs, not assumptions.

We also collaborated with classroom teachers to ensure the app aligns with Common Core Language Arts standards, making it a tool that bridges home therapy and school learning.

## What's Next

We're continuously improving FreeSpeech based on feedback from therapists, parents, and — most importantly — the children who use it. Upcoming features include expanded vocabulary sets, additional language support, and improved progress-tracking reports for clinicians.
