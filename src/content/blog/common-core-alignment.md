## Why Common Core Alignment Matters

For speech therapists and special educators, every tool they use in the classroom or clinic must justify its place. Time with students is precious, and any activity that doesn't connect to learning goals is time taken away from measurable progress.

That's why we built Common Core alignment into FreeSpeech from the ground up, not as an afterthought.

## Standards We Address

### CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.L.K.1 — Kindergarten Grammar

At the Kindergarten level, FreeSpeech focuses on helping children understand that sentences have structure. The subject-verb-object tile arrangement directly teaches children to place words in the correct grammatical order.

### CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.L.1.1 — Grade 1 Parts of Speech

Our vocabulary library categorises every tile by part of speech. Noun tiles, verb tiles, adjective tiles, and connector tiles are visually distinct, helping children internalise parts-of-speech concepts without memorising terminology.

### CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.L.2.1 — Grade 2 Expanded Sentences

By Grade 2, children are expected to use plural nouns, reflexive pronouns, and more complex sentence structures. FreeSpeech's progressive difficulty levels introduce these grammatical features incrementally.

### CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.1.4 — Speaking and Listening

FreeSpeech includes text-to-speech: after building a sentence, children can hear it spoken aloud. This connects written grammar to spoken language, meeting the Speaking and Listening strand of Common Core.

## How to Use This in Your Practice

We recommend starting with the Vocabulary Builder activity to assess which parts of speech a child already understands, then using the Sentence Constructor to introduce new grammatical patterns one at a time.

The built-in progress reports show you exactly which standards a child is practising, making IEP documentation straightforward.
